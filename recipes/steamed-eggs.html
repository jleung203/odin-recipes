Steamed Eggs


A big bowl of soft and silky eggs that slide right off from the spoon. It's like silken tofu's cousin!


Ingredients

2 eggs
1 cup warm water
1 green onion, chopped into small pieces
1/4 tsp salt
1/4 tsp white pepper
1/2 tsp chicken bouillon powder
1 tsp sesame oil
1 tsp soy sauce


Steps

  1. Place a steamer rack in the middle of a pan, and pour water into the pan until it is about 2 to 3 inches high. Let the water come to a boil.
  2. Crack the eggs into a bowl and add in salt and chicken bouillon powder. Mix together thoroughly.
  3. Pour 1/4 cup of the warm water into the egg mixture and mix until bubbles start to appear at the top. Then, pour the remaining 3/4 cups of warm water into the mixture, and continue to stir until bubbles form once again.
  4. Place a bowl in the pan and pour the egg mixture into the bowl. Remove the bubbles on the surface of the mixture with a spoon, and cover the pan with a lid.
  5. Leave on high heat until the water in the pan is boiling again. Once boiling, lower to medium heat and let the eggs cook for 8 minutes.
  6. Turn off the heat and keep the lid on. Let the eggs sit in the residual heat for 3 to 5 minutes.
  7. Remove the lid. Drizzle white pepper, sesame oil, soy sauce, and green onion pieces evenly across the top of the eggs. Enjoy!
